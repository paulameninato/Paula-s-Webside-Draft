Web Design for Artists

Promote Your Art Through a Professional Website Without Compromising Your Time in the Studio

As artists, in addition to creating our work, we have to promote our art if we want to create an arts career. Most of us have a day-job along with our studio practice, so marketing our work can feel overwhelming.

An artist website is one of your most essential promotional tools. Art curators and potential buyers regularly view artist's work online before investing in their work. Unfortunately, countless artists lose opportunities because they lack the technical resources to exhibit their work online in a professional manner.

Designing my first website was incredibly frustrating. I taught myself web design by experimenting until the website functioned how I needed it to. The whole process was quite stressful and gave me multiple migraines.

Thankfully, my hard work paid off. A colleague offered me the opportunity to curate monthly exhibitions at the East End Salon after showing them my portfolio via my website. This opportunity helped me promote my work in the heart of the arts district of Philadelphia. From East End, I was able to network and earn incredible opportunities to further my arts career.

Before graduating from university, I was offered solo shows at The Embassy of Argentina in Washington, DC, The National Real Estate Gallery, Tyler School of Art, and Georgian Court University!
